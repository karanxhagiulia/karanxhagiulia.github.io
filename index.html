A data nerd with a passion for data visualization!

This passion started with Brexit when Cambridge Analytica changed the course of Europe through data: I then volunteered in Nairobi, Kenya, and Cambridge Analytica used data again to alter people’s opinions during the elections in 2017. I became so fascinated with Ethics and human psychology, but it wasn’t until the pandemic that I started spending all my free time researching, understanding bias, ethics, and how they impact everyday life. After high school, I decided to work and travel: I volunteered, lived abroad (almost four years in Australia), but in the last few years I decided I wanted to study and dedicate myself to develop technical skills analyzing data, and now I can use SQL, Python, PowerBI, Tableau, Excel, and Data Science libraries in Python such as Pandas, SciKit-Learn, Numpy, Matplotlib, Plotly to produce accurate reports and data visualization from raw data to solid information, accessible to everyone. I think that’s where the power of data lies: improving people’s lives and creating something better for both society and businesses at the same time! I worked as a Store Manager in Retail and data was a powerful tool I still didn’t completely understand, but I could use my intuition to make changes for my store and improve our numbers. I am a very versatile person, I like to adapt to different situations, and I found, in the role as a data analyst, the perfect fit for my personality! This gets me extremely excited about the many ways I can use my skills in different sectors. All those years of experience in management and retail taught me how to be professional and to be empathetic at the same time: I know I can be a valuable asset at work because I’m eager to learn more and absorb everything I can from experienced people!
